A Legacy of Excellence,
A Future of Global Innovation

After a decade of successfully organizing the National Conference (NCMAT), Deccan Education Society’s NMITD, in collaboration with Smt. Jayshree Sharadchandra Kothari Business School and the University of Mumbai, is proud to mark a significant milestone in 2026. Celebrating 10 years of academic excellence and research innovation, our flagship conference now expands its footprint to the global stage as INCMAT 2026. Backed by the 140-year legacy of the Deccan Education Society, we continue our tradition of excellence by fostering international research collaborations and promoting interdisciplinary engagement.

Our 2026 theme, “New Horizons: Integrating AI, Business, and Sustainable Future,” addresses the evolving global landscape shaped by rapid technological advancement and the urgent need for sustainable development. INCMAT 2026 aims to convene scholars, industry experts, and policymakers from across the world to explore emerging trends in AI-enabled business models and resilient organizational growth. We invite you to join this global dialogue as we foster innovative solutions that contribute to long-term sustainability and global progress.

“The future belongs not to those who innovate alone, but to those who innovate responsibly and sustainably.”

Guidelines

To ensure a seamless peer-review and publication process for INCMAT 2026, all authors are requested to adhere to the following submission protocols.

Abstract Submission Guidelines


Authors are invited to submit their research abstracts for initial review. Please follow these steps:

  • Submission Channel: All abstracts must be submitted via email to ncmat@nmitd.edu.in.
  • Verification: Ensure that the names provided in the abstract match those on the registration form exactly.
  • Confirmation: Once accepted, the submitted details will be reflected in your formal acceptance mail.

Full Paper Submission Process


Upon abstract acceptance, the full paper submission process is managed through the Microsoft CMT system to ensure academic rigor and tracking:

  • System Usage: All papers must be uploaded through the official CMT portal (links provided below).
  • Account Setup: Authors must create a CMT account to manage their submissions and track review status.
  • Data Accuracy: Authors are responsible for the accuracy of the data and names provided during submission, as these will be used for certificates and journal publication.
  • Plagiarism Policy: All submissions will undergo a mandatory plagiarism check before being sent for peer review.

The Microsoft CMT service was used for managing the peer-reviewing process for this conference.
This service was provided for free by Microsoft and they bore all expenses, including costs for Azure cloud services as well as for software development and support.

Submission Guidelines


  • Manuscript Format
    •         - Font: Times New Roman
            - Font size:
                        -Title: 14 pt (bold)
                        -Headings: 12 pt (bold)
                        -Body text: 10–11 pt
            - Line spacing: 1.15
            - Margins: Standard (1 inch / 2.54 cm on all sides)


  • Length and File Size
    •        - Full paper length: 6000-8000 words (including references, tables, and figures)
           - Abstract length: 150–250 words
           - Keywords: 3–6
           - Maximum file size: 5 MB


  • Figures and Tables
    •        Figures and tables must be clearly labelled and numbered. Captions must be placed below figures and above tables.
            All figures must be of high resolution and embedded within the text.


  • Referencing and Citations
    •        References must follow the APA Style (7th Edition). All cited works must be included in the reference list.
